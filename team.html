Origin

The lab behind the unit.

A small team, one obsession: a machine that protects like a person would. It started with a single designer and a sketch of a visor.

Founder
Founder & Chief Designer
Hamza Inayat

Defined the look, the doctrine and the soul of VEYRONIS-01 — from the first sketch of the visor to the gold line down its spine. Every surface on the unit started on his desk.

Chief Roboticist
Dr. Adrian Cole

Turns drawings into a body that moves. Leads the mechanical design of the frame, the joints, and the forty-segment spinal lattice.

Head of Actuation & Movement
Eleanor Shaw

Owns how VEYRONIS-01 moves — the actuation that lets it hold something fragile and stop a slamming door with the same hands.

Lead — Cognition & Behaviour
Marcus Reed

Builds the part that reacts like a human. Leads the reasoning and behaviour core, and the protective directive that bounds everything it does.

Principal Materials Engineer
Sofia Marlow

Develops the obsidian composite and gold-threaded lattice — the materials that make the unit light, silent and almost unbreakable.

Systems Integration Lead
Daniel Frost

Makes every system speak to every other. Power, sensing and cognition, fused into one whole that reacts as a single body.

The intent

“We weren't building a product. We were building something that would stand its ground.”
